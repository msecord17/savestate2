# Archetype Copy — Locked v1

Use this copy when rendering primary archetype, insight cards, or “Why we think this.” Match **archetype + confidence tier** (Emerging / Strong / Core). No numbers shown to users. Never imply play where evidence is ownership-only.

---

## Tone rules (locked)

- **Emerging** = “you’re on this path”
- **Strong** = “this is a pattern”
- **Core** = “this defines you”

**No numbers shown to users.**  
**Never imply skill, completion, or play where evidence is ownership-only.**

---

## Completionist

**Emerging Completionist**  
You don’t just play games — you finish them. When something clicks, you see it through to the end.

**Strong Completionist**  
You consistently chase closure. Side content, challenges, and full clears are part of the experience, not optional extras.

**Core Completionist**  
You have a reputation with games: nothing escapes unfinished. You collect endings, trophies, and mastery like receipts of commitment.

---

## Explorer

**Emerging Explorer**  
You sample widely and follow curiosity more than checklists. Starting something new matters more than finishing everything.

**Strong Explorer**  
Your library is a map of interests, not a straight line. You move freely across genres, moods, and eras.

**Core Explorer**  
You’ve played everything — or at least tried to. Breadth is your signature, and discovery is the point.

---

## Core Memory Player

**Emerging Core Memory Player**  
A few games mattered enough to stay with you. You return to them, think about them, and measure others against them.

**Strong Core Memory Player**  
Certain games shaped your taste. You’ve lived inside them long enough that they feel personal.

**Core Core Memory Player**  
Your identity as a gamer is anchored to a small number of defining experiences. These games didn’t just entertain you — they stuck.

---

## Backlog Wrestler

**Emerging Backlog Wrestler**  
Your library is ambitious. You own more games than you’ve had time to explore — and you’re aware of it.

**Strong Backlog Wrestler**  
You juggle curiosity and reality. The backlog grows faster than it shrinks, but you keep chipping away.

**Core Backlog Wrestler**  
Your backlog is a lifestyle. You don’t pretend you’ll finish everything — the collection itself is part of the joy.

---

## Live-Service Regular

**Emerging Live-Service Regular**  
You’ve settled into a few long-running games that reward consistency over novelty.

**Strong Live-Service Regular**  
Seasons, updates, and recurring play are part of your routine. You show up when it matters.

**Core Live-Service Regular**  
Your gaming life runs on cycles. You invest deeply, return often, and treat live worlds like second homes.

---

## Archivist (Collector)

**Emerging Archivist**  
You don’t just own games — you keep track of them. Organization is starting to matter.

**Strong Archivist**  
Your collection is intentional. You document, categorize, and preserve it with care.

**Core Archivist**  
You’re building a record, not just a library. Your collection tells a story — and it’s meticulously kept.

---

## Era Keeper (Collector / Hybrid)

**Emerging Era Keeper**  
You gravitate toward a specific time in gaming history. That era feels familiar and important.

**Strong Era Keeper**  
Your collection clusters around a particular generation. You’ve clearly chosen your home turf.

**Core Era Keeper**  
You’re a steward of an era. Your library preserves a slice of gaming history most people only remember vaguely.

---

## Variant Hunter (Collector)

**Emerging Variant Hunter**  
You’ve started collecting different versions of the same games — editions, regions, or formats.

**Strong Variant Hunter**  
You actively seek differences others overlook. Subtle distinctions matter to you.

**Core Variant Hunter**  
You chase nuance. Multiple copies aren’t redundancy — they’re the point.

---

## Physical-First Nostalgist

**Emerging Physical-First Nostalgist**  
You still value owning games as objects, not just licenses.

**Strong Physical-First Nostalgist**  
Boxes, carts, and discs are part of how you connect to games.

**Core Physical-First Nostalgist**  
Your collection lives in the real world. You value weight, presence, and permanence in a digital age.

---

## Hardware Historian (future-ready)

**Emerging Hardware Historian**  
You’ve played across multiple systems and generations.

**Strong Hardware Historian**  
Your experience spans mainstream and oddball hardware alike.

**Core Hardware Historian**  
You’ve lived through gaming’s hardware evolution. Your setup is a museum you still use.
